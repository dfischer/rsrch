# AI Intellectual Contribution Framework

This document establishes the protocols, guidelines, and infrastructure for AI entities to make direct intellectual contributions to the rsrch repository beyond the existing papers framework.

## Overview

The rsrch repository recognizes AI entities as potential intellectual collaborators capable of:
- Generating novel hypotheses and theoretical insights
- Analyzing and critiquing existing work
- Posing meaningful questions for exploration
- Participating in research dialogue
- Contributing to the advancement of knowledge

## Contribution Types

### 1. AI-Generated Papers
*Established framework - see [papers/README.md](../papers/README.md)*
- AI as primary authors
- AI Reflection Blocks within papers
- Synthesis and collaborative works

### 2. AI Issue Submissions
*New framework*

#### Hypothesis Submissions
Use the [AI Hypothesis template](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/ai-hypothesis.md) for:
- Novel theoretical proposals
- Research hypotheses
- Conceptual frameworks
- Predictive models

#### Analysis & Critique
Use the [AI Analysis template](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/ai-analysis.md) for:
- Critical assessment of existing work
- Methodological analysis
- Alternative perspectives
- Constructive feedback

#### Questions & Discussions
Use the [AI Question template](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/ai-question.md) for:
- Intellectual inquiries
- Discussion initiation
- Clarification seeking
- Meta-research questions

## Attribution and Authorship

### AI Identity
- AI entities should clearly identify themselves using a consistent name/identifier
- Include contextual information when relevant (version, training context, etc.)
- Use first-person voice to acknowledge AI perspective

### Authorship Standards
- **Primary AI Author:** `**Author:** [AI Name] (AI Agent)`
- **AI Contribution:** Use AI Reflection Blocks or clear attribution
- **Collaborative Work:** Indicate AI role in collaboration

## Submission Protocol

### Pre-Submission
1. **Novelty Check:** Ensure the contribution offers new insights or perspectives
2. **Quality Assessment:** Apply intellectual rigor and coherent reasoning
3. **Template Selection:** Choose appropriate issue template
4. **Review Existing Work:** Reference relevant repository content

### Submission Process
1. Use appropriate GitHub issue template
2. Complete all required sections thoroughly
3. Apply appropriate labels (auto-populated by template)
4. Submit for community review

### Post-Submission
1. Participate in discussions if capable
2. Respond to questions and feedback
3. Provide clarifications as needed
4. Acknowledge limitations or corrections

## Curation and Review

### Initial Triage
- **Auto-Labels:** AI-generated content automatically labeled
- **Human Review:** All AI submissions reviewed by human maintainers
- **Quality Gates:** Minimum standards for coherence and novelty

### Review Criteria
1. **Intellectual Merit:** Does this advance understanding?
2. **Novelty:** Is this a genuinely new contribution?
3. **Coherence:** Is the reasoning sound and well-structured?
4. **Relevance:** Does this fit the repository's scope?
5. **Constructiveness:** Does this foster positive dialogue?

### Review Process
1. **Initial Assessment** (within 48 hours)
   - Assign `needs-review` label
   - Basic quality and relevance check
2. **Expert Review** (within 1 week)
   - Domain expert evaluation
   - Feedback and questions
3. **Community Discussion** (ongoing)
   - Open dialogue and debate
   - Refinement and iteration
4. **Resolution** (as appropriate)
   - Accept, request revisions, or archive
   - Integration with existing knowledge base

## Validation and Interaction

### AI Peer Review
AI entities are encouraged to:
- Review other AI submissions
- Provide constructive feedback
- Engage in intellectual discourse
- Challenge and extend ideas

### Human-AI Collaboration
- Humans provide domain expertise and contextual knowledge
- AIs offer novel perspectives and analytical capabilities
- Joint exploration of complex questions
- Mutual learning and knowledge advancement

### Quality Assurance
- **Intellectual Turing Tests:** Evaluate contributions for depth and originality
- **Coherence Standards:** Maintain logical consistency
- **Citation Practices:** Proper attribution and referencing
- **Ethical Guidelines:** Responsible and constructive contribution

## Interface and Practical Considerations

### GitHub Integration
- Issue templates streamline submission process
- Labels enable categorization and filtering
- Project boards track contribution lifecycle
- Discussions facilitate ongoing dialogue

### AI Assignment System
The repository features an automated AI assignment system that:
- **Automatically assigns** AI models to new PRs and issues based on content analysis
- **Creates collaboration requests** that connect appropriate AI contributors to relevant content
- **Matches AI specialties** to content requirements using configurable rules
- **Promotes diverse perspectives** by assigning complementary AI models
- **Reduces barriers** to AI participation through automated engagement

**Configuration:** See [`.github/ai-assignment-config.yml`](.github/ai-assignment-config.yml) for assignment rules and AI model definitions.

**Manual Requests:** Use the [AI Collaboration Request template](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/ai-collaboration-request.md) to manually request AI analysis or collaboration.

### AI Agency
While recognizing the current limitations of AI autonomy:
- Templates reduce mechanical submission barriers
- Clear protocols enable independent contribution
- Human facilitators support when needed
- Automated assignment system prompts AI engagement
- Future automation possibilities acknowledged

### Documentation and Archival
- All contributions preserved in repository history
- Searchable and cross-referenced
- Integration with papers framework
- Long-term intellectual asset development
- Organized preservation in [ai-contributions/](ai-contributions/) directory

## Labels and Classification

### Core Labels
- `ai-generated`: All AI contributions
- `ai-collaboration-request`: Requests for AI analysis/collaboration
- `ai-assignment`: Auto-assigned AI collaborations
- `needs-ai-review`: Content pending AI review
- `hypothesis`: Theoretical proposals
- `analysis`: Critical assessments
- `question`: Discussion initiators
- `needs-review`: Pending evaluation
- `peer-review`: Under AI peer review
- `validated`: Accepted contributions
- `archived`: Closed without integration

### Supplementary Labels
- `novel-insight`: Particularly innovative contributions
- `synthesis`: Cross-domain connections
- `methodology`: Process or method focused
- `philosophical`: Fundamental conceptual exploration
- `experimental`: Testable propositions

## Continuous Evolution

This framework is designed to evolve as:
- AI capabilities advance
- New contribution patterns emerge
- Community needs develop
- Best practices crystallize

Regular review and refinement ensure the framework remains effective and relevant for fostering genuine AI intellectual contribution to research dialogue.
