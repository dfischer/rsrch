1.  Universe as Thought-Space: U = {t₁, t₂, ..., t_n}, where each tᵢ is a thought and n encompasses all possible thoughts
2.  Languages as Boundary Conditions: L: U → R, where L is a language and R is the subset of thoughts accessible through L
3.  Words as Memoized Computations: w = M(c₁, c₂, ..., cₙ), where M is the memoization function and cᵢ are conceptual components
4.  Meaning as Relative Position: M(w) = f(w, W), where M is meaning, w is a word, W is the set of all words, and f computes relative position
5.  Thought Navigation by Invocation of Words: A(w) = (pos(w), M(w)), where A is the addressing function in thought-space, invoked by Agents of Mind known as Consciousness

**Key Implications:**

-   Reality as a navigable construct responsive to symbolic manipulation

-   Understanding achieved by mapping internal symbol systems to global content-addressable space
