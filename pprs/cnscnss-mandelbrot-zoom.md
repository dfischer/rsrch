Proposition: Consciousness can be analogized to the act of zooming in on the Mandelbrot set, where each zoom reveals more detail and complexity.

Formal Setup:

P: Let P represent the set of all possible perceptions, an infinite and complex space akin to the Mandelbrot set.
C: Define C as the function representing consciousness, which operates on P.
A: Let A be a function representing attention, which selects elements from P.
Z: Define Z as a function that increases the detail or resolution of any subset of P.

Formal Argument:

Attention as Selection:
A: P → P', where P' is a subset of P. Here, A acts as a filter or selector, choosing which perceptions to focus on.
Zooming in Detail:
Z: P' → P'', where Z takes the selected subset P' and reveals more detail, akin to zooming in on a fractal. P'' contains finer details or deeper insights than P'.
Consciousness as Composite Function:
C = Z ∘ A, where ∘ denotes function composition. This means consciousness C first selects a subset of perceptions through A and then zooms in on these perceptions with Z.
Infinite Detail and Self-Similarity:
For any P' in P, Z(P') reveals more detail, suggesting an infinite depth of perception, much like the Mandelbrot set where each zoom reveals new structures.

By this model, consciousness operates similarly to zooming in on the Mandelbrot set. Each act of attention (A) followed by a deepening of perception (Z) uncovers more complexity and detail in our experience, suggesting that consciousness explores the perceptual field with an ever-increasing resolution.
